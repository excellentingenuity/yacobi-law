South Carolina Adoption Laws

The Code of Laws of South Carolina describes the adoption process in this state and covers many important procedural requirements and necessary safeguards to insure the protection of the adoptive couple, the biological parents, and most importantly, the child to be adopted.

The code section on adoptions establishes fair and reasonable procedures for the adoption of children and provides for the well-being of the adopted child, with full recognition of the interdependent needs and interests of the biological parents and the adoptive parents. However, when the interests of a child and an adult are in conflict, the conflict must be resolved in favor of the child. Children may be adopted by or placed for adoption with residents of South Carolina only, except in unusual or exceptional circumstances.

This section of the South Carolina code is quite extensive, and qualified legal counsel is advised for all parties involved in the adoption process.
